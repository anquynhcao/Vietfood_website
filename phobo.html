Pho Bo

Difficulty rating:

Medium

Introduction

Phá»Ÿ bÃ², a quintessential dish of Vietnamese cuisine, is a hearty and aromatic beef noodle soup that embodies the essence of Vietnamese flavors. Steeped in history and tradition, this beloved dish consists of tender slices of beef, rice noodles, and a fragrant broth infused with spices such as star anise, cinnamon, and cloves.

The ingredients are for 2-people portion.

Ingredients

Ingredients Unit Amount
Pho noodle (raw) gram 500
Beef sirloin gram 500
Onion/shallots - 2
Green Onion - 2
Coriander gram 100
Ginger gram 50
Beef bone gram 700
Dried cinnamon, cardamom, tangerine peel, star anise, cloves, coriander seeds pieces 2 each
Sugar tbsp 4
Salt tbsp 2
Water liter 3
Fish sauce tbsp 2

Instructions

  • Peel and slice onions. Finely chop coriander roots. Separate green and white parts of onions, then finely chop the green parts. Clean and drain accompanying vegetables
  • Preparing the Pho Spices: Place dried cinnamon, anise, orange peel, cloves, and coriander seeds in a cloth bag and tie tightly. These spices are crucial for pho's flavor.
  • Making the Beef Broth: Thoroughly clean beef bones. Boil a pot of water and briefly parboil the bones to remove any odors. Bake the parboiled bones with ginger and shallots until golden. Then, immediately place them in an ice water bath to enhance flavor. Simmer baked bones, spice bag, onion, and ginger for 5-6 hours to make a flavorful broth. Remove ingredients, strain the broth, season with salt and sugar and fish sauce to taste.
  • Presentation and Enjoyment: Clean and thinly slice beef sirloin and brisket. Briefly blanch white parts of onions and pho noodles in boiling water. Arrange green onions and noodles in a bowl, top with beef sirloin, coriander, shallots, and chili. Pour hot broth over the ingredients. Serve with herbs, chili sauce, and hoisin sauce. Enjoy your pho!
