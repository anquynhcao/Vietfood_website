Nem cuon

Difficulty rating:

Easy

Introduction

Nem cuốn, also known as Vietnamese fresh spring rolls, offers a refreshing and flavorful twist to traditional spring rolls. Originating from the vibrant culinary landscape of Vietnam, nem cuốn embodies the essence of Vietnamese cuisine with its light, healthy, and vibrant ingredients...

Ingredients

Ingredients Unit Amount
Shrimp gram 400
Pork belly or pork leg gram 300
Fresh rice vermicelli gram 400
Vietnamese pork sausage (giò lụa) (optional) gram 200
Herbs (coriander or Vietnamese perilla) gram 100
Lettuce head 5
Lime, fish sauce, vinegar, chili, garlic - to taste

Instructions

  • Boil the pork belly until cooked, then thinly slice it.
  • Boil the shrimp until cooked, peel, and cut them in half lengthwise.
  • Slice the Vietnamese pork sausage lengthwise.
  • Clean the lettuce leaves individually; pick and clean the herbs, chives.
  • Place a rice paper on a flat surface, put the shrimp on, place it at the top of the rice paper. Then add the pork, vermicelli, and various herbs on top, roll tightly from the top.
  • Fold the edges of both ends so that the filling does not fall out, then add some chive leaves lengthwise, allowing the chive leaves to slightly extend beyond, then roll to the end of the rice paper.
  • Prepare the dipping sauce with a ratio of 1:1:1:5, meaning 1 tablespoon fish sauce, 1 tablespoon sugar, 1 tablespoon vinegar, 5 tablespoons water. This is the most balanced ratio to give you a well-balanced, harmonious dipping sauce. However, you can adjust it according to your taste. Put fish sauce in a bowl, add sugar, vinegar, and stir well. Add water, continue stirring until the sugar dissolves, then add minced garlic and chili.
  • That's it! Dip the spring rolls in the dipping sauce and enjoy!
